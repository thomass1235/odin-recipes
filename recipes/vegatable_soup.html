Vegetable soup

Description

A hearty soup suitable for people with any dietary requirements, perfect for a cold winter's day! This recipe is not only delicious but highly customisable, cheap, quick, and easy. Any of the main vegetables can be substituted for other, similar vegetables depending on your preferences or dietary requirements (and once you've made it a few times, you may gain the confidence to experiment more with the ingredients to make your own wonderful creations!)

You can also use this as an opportunity to use up any leftover ingredients you have in your fridge, helping reduce food waste and creating delicious concoctions you may never have considered before in the process. So next time it's looking a little wet or blustery outside, and all you want to do is stay indoors, consider making this recipe to warm your heart and your stomach, and help Mother Earth in the process!

Ingredients

Makes approx. 2 servings

  • 200g chopped vegetables (such as carrot, celery, leek, onion, etc.)
  • 300g diced potatoes
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 700ml stock (any kind you have, vegetable if you wish recipe to remain vegetarian)
  • Creme fraiche and fresh herbs to serve (optional)
  • Fresh bread to serve (optional, but highly recommended!)

Steps

  1. Combine the vegetables, potato and oil in a saucepan and fry for a few minutes to soften them slightly.
  2. Add the stock to the pan and allow to simmer until the vegetables have softened (approximately 10-15 minutes, but check occasionally so they do not become mushy).
  3. Blend the mixture until smooth (either using a stick blender, or transferring the mixture carefully to a food mixer).
  4. Transfer the soup to two bowls (or as many bowls as you wish to make portions you believe to be appropriate for you!), then optionally top with creme fraiche and herbs, and enjoy (hopefully with some warm, fresh bread and butter).
